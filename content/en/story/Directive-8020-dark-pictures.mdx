## Gameplay Systems You Should Learn Before Your First Run

You can treat the game as two intertwined loops:

1. **Narrative loop**: conversations, moral choices, branching scenes  
2. **Survival loop**: movement tension, threat recognition, critical timing

Mastering both loops is likely the difference between “some survivors” and a total collapse run.

### Priority systems for beginners

| System | What To Do Early | Mistake To Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| Choice consequences | Commit to a roleplay style for consistency | Randomly switching moral logic every scene |
| Exploration | Search thoroughly for lore and conspiracy clues | Sprinting main path and missing context |
| Identity reading | Watch speech habits and behavior cues | Trusting appearances only |
| Turning Points | Use after first completion to test branches | Rewinding constantly on first blind run |
| Team relationship management | Preserve trust unless evidence says otherwise | Burning alliances too early |

The new Turning Points feature deserves special mention. It gives you a branching-map way to revisit pivotal decisions without replaying every hour from scratch. For anthology fans, this is one of the most valuable quality-of-life additions in 2026.

> **Warning:** Turning Points is ideal for experimentation, but overusing it can flatten tension. Save heavy rewinds for a second or third run if you want the strongest horror pacing.

### Exploration and collectibles

Collectibles now do more than fill a menu. They appear to expand series lore, explain Earth’s downfall timeline, and reveal possible corporate conspiracy threads tied to the mission. If you usually skip optional rooms, don’t do that here—**Directive 8020 dark pictures** seems designed so side information improves both story clarity and decision quality.
