## How to Follow Cast Updates Without Getting Burned by Rumors

The fastest way to get bad info is relying on clipped social posts that crop out context. Use a verification workflow instead.

### Reliable Verification Checklist

| Source Type | Reliability | What to Verify |
|---|---|---|
| **Developer/publisher post** | **Very High** | Date, full wording, attached media |
| **Official storefront update** | **High** | Cast/credits section changes |
| **Major games media interview** | Medium-High | Direct quotes vs paraphrasing |
| Unverified social leak | Low | Usually lacks corroborating evidence |
| Aggregator repost | Low | May recycle old speculation |

Follow these steps:

1. Check if the claim appears on an official channel in 2026.  
2. Confirm whether the statement says “featured,” “cast,” or “cameo.”  
3. Compare at least two independent reputable sources.  
4. Save screenshots of official statements for later cross-checking.
