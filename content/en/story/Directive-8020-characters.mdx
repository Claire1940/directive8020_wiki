## How to Read Directive 8020 Characters During Gameplay

When the game launches, most players will lose characters on their first run because they treat scenes as cinematic instead of interactive intelligence tests. Follow this approach to improve survival odds.

### 1) Track relationship pressure, not just dialogue tone

A “kind” line isn’t always optimal. Sometimes a firm line stabilizes command and prevents future panic.

### 2) Separate fear reactions from betrayal clues

In horror games, frightened behavior can look suspicious. Don’t assume panic equals guilt.

### 3) Use role logic under stress

If someone is a pilot, medic, or command authority, ask: what would this role prioritize right now?

### 4) Plan for branch risk in every major choice

Before selecting, quickly estimate:  
- Who loses trust?  
- Who gains control?  
- Does this split the crew?

| Decision Moment | Low-Risk Choice Pattern | High-Risk Choice Pattern |
|---|---|---|
| Command disagreement | **Calm assertive response** | Emotional challenge in public |
| Unknown threat sighting | **Verify + regroup** | Solo pursuit under panic |
| Crew accusation scene | **Ask for evidence** | Immediate blame commitment |
| Evacuation timing | **Team-based sequencing** | Heroic split without backup |

> **Tip:** In your first run, prioritize crew cohesion over “bold hero” choices. Cohesion often unlocks safer midgame branches.
