## Best Setup for First Playthrough on Switch 2

Once **Directive 8020 switch 2** is in your library, your settings matter more than many players expect. Horror loses impact if the image is too bright, too blurry, or if audio cues are buried.

### Recommended launch-day settings priorities

| Setting Area | Recommended Starting Point | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Brightness/Gamma | Slightly below neutral, then tune per dark-room test | Protects contrast without losing critical detail |
| Motion Blur | Low or Off (preference-based) | Keeps camera pans cleaner in tight spaces |
| Film Grain | Low | Maintains mood without obscuring fine detail |
| Headphones Mix | On (if available) | Improves directional cues and tension |
| Subtitle Style | Medium size, high contrast | Keeps dialogue readable during stress moments |

### Control and comfort optimization

1. **Calibrate stick sensitivity** before chapter one  
2. **Test QTE responsiveness** in early low-risk sequences  
3. **Use headphones** for ship ambience and threat localization  
4. **Play in a dim room** to maximize environmental lighting contrast  
5. **Set session checkpoints** between chapters to avoid fatigue decisions

> **Warning:** Don’t over-brighten the game just to “see everything.” That can flatten visual tension and reduce environmental storytelling.
